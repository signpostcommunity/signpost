About

I'm a certified interpreter with over 12 years of experience working across medical, legal, and international conference settings. I specialize in ASL and International Sign and hold dual certification from RID (USA) and EFSLI (EU). I grew up in a Deaf household, which gives me a deep cultural connection to the community I serve.

I'm fluent in both English and Spanish and can bridge communication across multiple language pairs. I'm comfortable with high-pressure, fast-paced environments and regularly interpret at UN-affiliated events.

In addition to interpreting, I offer event coordination — I am available to coordinate interpreter teams for complex and/or large-scale events (conferences, summits, multi-day institutional events, and more). This includes interpreter scheduling, team briefings, relay interpreting logistics, and on-site coordination.

Our story

Built by people who
know this problem firsthand.

signpost grew out of a conversation between two best friends — a certified interpreter and a Deaf mental health professional — about something they'd both been frustrated by for years.

Finding the right interpreter is harder than it should be. Deaf people often have to rely on whoever an agency assigns, with limited visibility into that person's signing style, cultural background, or domain expertise — and without much say in the matter.

We built signpost to change that. A place where interpreters can present themselves fully and honestly, and where the Deaf community can make informed, empowered choices about who they work with.

The people behind it

Regina McGinnis
Co-founder · Deaf Professional

A Deaf mental health professional with 20 years of experience. Regina brings the perspective of someone who has spent decades navigating the interpreter booking process — and who has felt the impact of poor fit, limited transparency, and lack of choice. Her voice is at the center of how signpost is designed.

Molly Sano-Mahgoub
Co-founder · Interpreter

A certified interpreter with 18 years of professional experience and an RID NIC-Master certification. Molly has seen firsthand how difficult it can be for Deaf individuals to find interpreters who are a genuine fit — particularly for specialized, high-stakes settings. More about her professional background coming soon.

Why we built this

"We've had countless conversations about this over the years. As an interpreter, I've watched clients get assigned someone who wasn't the right fit — wrong specialization, different signing style, no shared cultural context. As a Deaf professional, Regina has lived that experience directly, especially in high-stakes mental health settings where the interpreter relationship really matters."

"signpost isn't about replacing the existing ecosystem. It's about adding something that's been missing: a way for people to see, choose, and connect with interpreters directly — with full transparency on both sides."

— Molly & Regina
